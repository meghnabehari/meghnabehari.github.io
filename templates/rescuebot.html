RescueBot

In recent years, natural disasters including hurricanes, earthquakes, and forest fires have increased significantly, causing serious devastation. In these natural disaster settings, rescuers are responsible for locating survivors and moving them to safety. They must manually search through rubble, debris, or flood conditions to locate survivors, which poses risks to their health and safety. To address this problem, an approach is needed to increase safety for both rescuers and those in need of rescue. In this project, I developed an assistive rescue-aid platform called RescueBot. RescueBot travels autonomously through a disaster-stricken area, searching for human presence using infrared thermography and concurrently assessing any dangerous obstacles obstructing the path. Once the area has been assessed, a route which maximizes efficiency and safety is produced using a shortest path algorithm. This route provides a path to safety and shelter, and helps rescuers identify an efficient route to rescue people in the quickest and safest manner. The body of Rescuebot is a multi-rotor copter called a quadcopter, consisting of a carbon fiber frame and 5 inch propellers. To control the motion of the UAV there is an Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) autopilot, called ArduPilot Mega (APM). The APM software, called Mission Planner, is based upon the same software I used to program the thermal sensor, and comes with an easy-to-use UI to aid in set-up. I used Mission Planner’s open source firmware to set-up and initialize my drone.

To understand RescueBot’s application in the rescue aid process, I spoke with several representatives from The Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA). They confirmed that a device such as RescueBot would be applicable to their work, and could help assist in the rescue aid process to make it safer and more efficient.
