Universal UAV Data Pipeline

I conducted this project as a part of a summer internship at Near Earth Autonomy, a tech company focusing on autonomous flight, under the mentorship of engineer Lauren Coblenz.

Near Earth Autonomy collects large amounts of data from each of their UAV devices, and it takes a lot of time and effort to organize and sort through each data type. To address this problem and reduce the amount of manpower it takes to process this data, I created a modular, all-encompassing tool that can process data, apply to all machines tested at Near Earth, as well as improve on the interfaces that are currently being used. The platform collects and supports the data collected by the UAVs and includes support for images, photos, point cloud files, kml files, and bag file. The GUI that I created was tested with a range of projects at Near Earth Autonomy, and was used for both real-time data collection, as well as post-processing.
